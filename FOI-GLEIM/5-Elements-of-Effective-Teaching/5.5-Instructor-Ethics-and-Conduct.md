## Instructor Ethics and Conduct

1) In addition to universally applicable characteristics of effective instructing, an instructor **also carries the added responsibility of shaping aviation citizens**
    - The following instructor _principles_ outline the values and responsibilities that define a good aviation citizen _**(RADARS)**_:
        - **R**espectful of flight privilege
        - **A**cts with responsibility and courtesy
        - **D**evelops/exercises good judgement in decision making
        - **A**ccountability for actions
        - **R**ecognizes/manages risk effectively
        - **S**afety is the number one priority
            - Seeks proficiency in aircraft control
            - Confident in a variety of flight situations 
            - Appropriate resource management
    - A formal code of ethics promotes
        - **Safety**
        - **Good judgement**
        - **Ethical behavior**
        - **Personal responsibility**
